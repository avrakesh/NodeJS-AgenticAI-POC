**Executive Summary**  
The defect data highlights critical focus areas across departments, with the Product, Customer Experience, and Sales teams showing the highest defect counts and customer impact. High-priority defects are significant, underlining the need for targeted regression testing. Although repeated issues appear minimal, volume and severity indicate stability risks. Customer-facing defects dominate key business functions, which could affect service quality and reputation. Focused testing and swift remediation in these areas are essential to mitigate business risks.

---

**Key Insights**  
- **Top 3 High-Risk Departments:**  
  1. **Product**: Highest defect volume (total 140+) and broad user impact including all customers and dwellings; considerable high-priority defects likely within this group.  
  2. **Customer Experience**: High defect count (53), mostly affecting customers and staff; critical because it directly influences user satisfaction.  
  3. **Sales**: Notable defects (47) with mixed user impact including all customers and staff, indicating business-critical transactional risks.  

- **Stability Concerns:**  
  - No repeated issues reported; however, high defect counts suggest potential instability that may not yet be reflected as repeat defects. Close monitoring recommended.  

- **Severity Distribution:**  
  - Total defects: 227, with 79 high-priority indicating over one-third represent serious issues demanding immediate attention.  
  - Low-priority defects (148) remain significant and could contribute to operational inefficiencies if unattended.

---

**User Impact Analysis**  
- **Departments Affecting Most Users:**  
  - Product defects impact all customers, dwellings, suppliers, and staff—broad user segments increase business exposure.  
  - Customer Experience impacts some and all customers plus staff, directly influencing service delivery and customer retention.  
  - Sales defects also affect various user groups, including all customers and staff, risking transaction integrity.  

- **Business Risk Explanation:**  
  - Defects affecting all customers or dwellings pose high financial and reputation risks.  
  - Staff-impacted defects can reduce operational efficiency and delay issue resolution.  
  - Supplier-impacted defects (notably in Finance and Product) could affect partner relations and supply chain reliability.

---

**Regression Testing Recommendations**  
- **Priority Areas:**  
  - Product, Customer Experience, and Sales departments should be prioritized due to defect volume, severity, and user reach.  
- **Suggested Focus Scenarios:**  
  - End-to-end customer workflows in Product and Customer Experience, including interfaces touching all customers and dwellings.  
  - Sales transaction processes impacting multiple user segments.  
  - Cross-departmental interactions involving suppliers and staff areas to ensure stability.
